II

Existing

Due to current limitations listed below, smartphone users could really benefit from a mobile app that replaces the current CNAS cards, making them obsolete for this segment of the population:

  1. the only piece of paper that you don't have to use anymore is the certificate of employment letter - other than that, you still get everything on paper (prescriptions, referrals for specialist care, and so on)
  2. if you don't have the card with you, but you have the prescription (that you got from your family physician via the health card), you can't get your medicine or, best case scenario, you'll have to pay it in full
  3. if you travel outside your county and you can't get to your physician for a specialist referral, you can't use the National Healthcare Services for free (unless one goes to the ER), although you are insured
  4. you have no knowledge of what's been added to your database related to your health card - only medical professionals have access to your data
  5. your PIN code gets overheard by anyone in proximity, because by default all health professionals ask for it aloud, they don't let you type it
  6. if you lose the card or it gets stolen you have to wait for anothor one to be emitted, during which you can't make use of it
Proposal

Any data that you need can be stored in a database accessible through the app, there is no need for cards or paper. Just as medical professionals currently use a card reader, they would use a scanner for your phone. The app would organize a patient's healthcare needs according to each use case:

  1. users want to view their personal data saved in the system
  2. users want to send a request to their family physician - for a specialist referral, an appointment for a checkup, or others
  3. users are at the pharmacy and they need to buy medication using a prescription from their doctor
  4. users are at the specialist physician with a referral from their GP
  5. users are at a lab/clinic/hospital to get medical imaging or other tests
  6. users need to access their medical history, bought prescriptions, and so on
